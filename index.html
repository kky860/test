UTORO
ART FESTIVAL
2025

The Utoro Art Festival 2025 in Kyoto highlights Utoro Village and Zainichi Koreans' resilient spirit—a community that navigated hardship to foster coexistence. Through contemporary art, it explores 'migration,' 'habitation,' and 'coexistence,' sharing this profound narrative with global audiences.

ウトロ・アート
フェスティバル
2025

京都で開催される「ウトロ・アートフェスティバル2025」は、ウトロを題材に、在日コリアンの強い精神と、困難を乗り越えて共生を育んできたコミュニティの物語に焦点を当てます。 現代アートを通じて「移動」「暮らし」「共生」をテーマに、この奥深い物語を世界中の観客と分かち合います。
About

The inaugural Utoro Art Festival (UAF2025), a celebration of contemporary art, kicks off on October 10, 2025, and runs until November 10, 2025. This month-long event will span multiple venues across Kyoto City and Uji City, Japan, offering a rich array of exhibitions, performances, lectures, film screening, and symposium. UAF2025 aims to transform the city into a vibrant and meaningful artistic hub, centered around the compelling themes of migration, habitation, and hope. We'll specifically explore how the global phenomenon of migration has shaped our past and will continue to redefine our future. We eagerly anticipate this journey.

Venues Utoro Peace Memorial Museum
The Utoro Peace Memorial Museum in Uji, Kyoto, honors the lives and struggles of Zainichi Korean structurally mobilized during Japanese occupation. It conveys messages of peace and human rights through the history of residents who defended their hometown, Utoro amidst discrimination and hardship.

Venues Goethe-Institut Villa Kamogawa
The Goethe-Institut is the globally active cultural institute of the Federal Republic of Germany.Goethe-Institut Villa Kamogawa in Kyoto offers 3-month residencies for German and European artists to create in Japan. It fosters project realization, new ideas, inspiration, and lasting ties with Japan's cultural scene. Villa Kamogawa is the Goethe-Institut's first artist residency and has been open since 2011, to mark the 150th anniversary of German-Japanese relations.

Venues Doshisha University
Doshisha University founded in 1875 by J.H. Neesima, embodies an "education of conscience." It fosters individuals valuing freedom, human rights, and global awareness. Its Imadegawa Campus, with Meiji-era architecture and YUN Dong-ju monuments, symbolizes a rich legacy of cultural exchange that defines Doshisha today.

Code of Conduct
＊Internal Gender Guidelines
Utoro Art Festival 2025 seeks to overcome discrimination against social minorities and aims to build solidarity and coexistence for the future. To foster mutual respect among all participants, we propose the following Code of Conduct and Checklist.
Preventive Guidelines
1. We do not engage in discriminatory or exclusionary language or behavior based on race, ethnicity, nationality, gender, age, appearance, sexual orientation, disability, cultural background, language, religion, or migration status.
2. We use respectful language toward one another.
3. We aim to challenge and change hierarchical
cultures based on fixed gender roles, age, or status.
4. We acknowledge that comments about someone’s body or appearance—even when intended as compliments—can be perceived as evaluative. Participants have the right to express discomfort and request the immediate cessation of sexually humiliating or sexist jokes.
5. We do not ask intrusive questions about others’ private matters, particularly regarding sexuality, romantic relationships, family, health, or religion.
6. We avoid unnecessary physical contact. If any contact causes discomfort, we will immediately stop and offer a sincere apology.
7. We must obtain explicit consent before taking or sharing photographs or videos of others.
Guidelines for Responding to Incidents
8. In cases where sexual harassment or assault is suspected, both victims and witnesses are encouraged to speak up and seek help immediately.
9. Anyone accused of sexual misconduct must stop the behavior immediately and offer an apology without making excuses.
10. When UAF2025 receives a report of sexual violence, we will listen with care regardless of the content and provide the victim with necessary information and support. (Reports can be submitted in writing, by phone, electronically, or in person.)
UAF2025 Response Protocol
11. UAF2025 will make every effort to protect the confidentiality and safety of victims and witnesses. No identifying information will be disclosed without the consent of the victim or their representative.
12. Upon receiving a report, the victim and accused will be promptly separated.
13. In accordance with the procedures of the UAF2025 Executive Committee, we will take disciplinary action against the perpetrator without delay.
14. The checklist will be regularly shared and reviewed to ensure the Code of Conduct is upheld at all times.
